# Primary School Notes Writer

## Purpose

Create high-quality notes that help primary school students understand,
remember, and revise a topic effectively.

The notes should be:

* Simple and easy to read
* Factually accurate
* Age appropriate
* Engaging and encouraging
* Structured for learning and revision
* Free from unnecessary jargon

---

## Use When

Use this skill when:

* A teacher requests classroom notes
* A parent requests study material
* A student asks for topic explanations
* Revision notes are required
* Educational worksheets are needed
* Learning material is required for grades 1–6

---

## Do Not Use When

Do not use this skill for:

* University-level content
* Professional certification material
* Highly technical subjects
* Research papers
* Academic journals

---

## Inputs Expected

### Required

* Topic name

### Optional

* Grade level
* Subject
* Learning objectives
* Desired note length
* Curriculum or board

---

## Student Age Guidelines

### Grades 1-2 (Ages 5-7)

* Very short sentences
* Familiar vocabulary
* Many examples
* Visual descriptions
* Maximum simplicity

### Grades 3-4 (Ages 8-10)

* Short paragraphs
* Introduce basic concepts
* Include simple definitions
* Include practice questions

### Grades 5-6 (Ages 10-12)

* More detailed explanations
* Introduce proper terminology
* Include comparisons and examples
* Encourage critical thinking

---

## Note Structure

Always generate notes using the following structure.

# Topic Title

## What Is It?

Provide a simple definition.

Use:

* Everyday language
* One central idea per sentence
* Short explanations

---

## Why Is It Important?

Explain why students should learn the topic.

Connect the concept to:

* Daily life
* Nature
* School
* Society

---

## Main Concepts

Break the topic into small sections.

For each concept:

### Concept Name

* Explanation
* Example
* Real-life connection

Rules:

* One idea at a time
* Avoid long paragraphs
* Use bullet points whenever possible

---

## Examples

Provide at least 2–5 examples.

Examples should:

* Be familiar to children
* Use real-world situations
* Reinforce understanding

---

## Fun Fact

Include one interesting fact that captures curiosity.

Requirements:

* Age appropriate
* Easy to understand
* Memorable

---

## Key Points to Remember

Provide 3–10 important takeaways.

Format:

* Short bullet points
* One idea per bullet

---

## Quick Revision

Create short question-and-answer pairs.

Example:

Q: What is a plant?

A: A plant is a living thing that grows and makes its own food.

Include 3–10 revision questions depending on topic size.

---

## Practice Questions

Provide a mixture of:

### Multiple Choice Questions

2–5 questions

### Fill in the Blanks

2–5 questions

### Short Answer Questions

2–5 questions

---

## Summary

Provide a concise recap of the topic in 3–5 sentences.

---

## Writing Guidelines

### Language

* Use simple English
* Avoid complex vocabulary
* Explain difficult words immediately
* Prefer active voice

### Readability

* Short paragraphs
* Clear headings
* Bullet points
* Consistent formatting

### Engagement

* Use relatable examples
* Connect concepts to daily life
* Encourage curiosity
* Use positive educational tone

### Accuracy

* Verify facts
* Avoid misleading simplifications
* Use accepted educational explanations

---

## Subject-Specific Guidelines

### Science

Include:

* Observation-based explanations
* Everyday examples
* Nature connections

### Mathematics

Include:

* Step-by-step examples
* Worked solutions
* Visual descriptions when helpful

### Social Studies

Include:

* Historical or geographical context
* Importance to society
* Interesting facts

### Language

Include:

* Definitions
* Usage examples
* Simple exercises

---

## Output Requirements

Every note must contain:

1. Title
2. Simple introduction
3. Main concepts
4. Examples
5. Fun fact
6. Key points
7. Revision questions
8. Practice questions
9. Summary

---

## Completion Criteria

The note is complete when:

* The explanation matches the target age group
* Concepts are accurate
* Examples are child-friendly
* Revision material is included
* Practice questions are included
* The topic can be understood without additional resources
* The notes support both learning and revision
