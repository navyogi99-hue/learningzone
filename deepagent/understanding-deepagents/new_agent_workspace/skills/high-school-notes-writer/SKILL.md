# High School Notes Writer

## Purpose

Create comprehensive, accurate, and exam-oriented notes that help
high school students:

* Understand concepts deeply
* Prepare for examinations
* Improve retention
* Build problem-solving skills
* Connect theory with real-world applications

---

## Use When

Use this skill when:

* Students need study notes
* Teachers need classroom material
* Parents need learning resources
* Revision guides are required
* Exam preparation material is needed
* Concept explanations are requested

---

## Do Not Use When

Do not use this skill for:

* University-level coursework
* Academic research papers
* Professional certifications
* Advanced graduate-level topics

---

## Inputs Expected

### Required

* Topic name

### Optional

* Grade/Class
* Subject
* Education board or curriculum
* Learning objectives
* Desired note length
* Exam focus level

---

## Student Level Guidelines

### Grades 7-8

* Introduce foundational concepts
* Use clear explanations
* Include practical examples
* Limit technical complexity

### Grades 9-10

* Introduce formal terminology
* Build conceptual understanding
* Include examination-style questions
* Emphasize applications

### Grades 11-12

* Provide deeper explanations
* Include analytical thinking
* Cover advanced concepts
* Highlight exam-relevant details

---

# Standard Note Structure

# Topic Title

## Learning Objectives

Students should understand:

* Objective 1
* Objective 2
* Objective 3

---

## Introduction

Provide:

* Definition
* Background
* Context
* Importance

Length:

* 1–3 paragraphs

---

## Key Terminology

| Term   | Definition |
| ------ | ---------- |
| Term 1 | Meaning    |
| Term 2 | Meaning    |

Include all important vocabulary students must know.

---

## Core Concepts

Break the topic into logical sections.

### Concept 1

#### Explanation

Detailed explanation.

#### Example

Relevant example.

#### Real-World Application

How the concept is used.

---

### Concept 2

Repeat same structure.

---

## Diagram Description (When Applicable)

If the topic benefits from visuals:

* Describe the diagram
* Label important parts
* Explain what students should observe

Examples:

* Human heart
* Cell structure
* Water cycle
* Electric circuit

---

## Important Formulas (When Applicable)

For mathematics and science:

### Formula Name

Formula:

Explanation of variables.

Units where applicable.

Example calculation.

---

## Worked Examples

Provide step-by-step solutions.

Requirements:

* Show every major step
* Explain reasoning
* Highlight common mistakes

---

## Real-World Applications

Explain how the topic applies to:

* Daily life
* Industry
* Technology
* Society
* Environment

Students retain information better when connected to reality.

---

## Common Misconceptions

List common mistakes students make.

Example:

Misconception:
"Speed and velocity are the same."

Correction:
"Velocity includes direction while speed does not."

---

## Exam Focus

Highlight:

* Frequently tested concepts
* Important definitions
* Common exam questions
* Typical marking points

Format:

### Remember

* Point 1
* Point 2
* Point 3

---

## Key Points Summary

Provide concise revision bullets.

Requirements:

* 5–15 points
* Exam-focused
* High information density

---

## Quick Revision Questions

### Very Short Answer

5–10 questions

Example:

Q: What is photosynthesis?

A: The process by which green plants make food using sunlight.

---

## Practice Questions

### Multiple Choice Questions

5–10 questions

### Fill in the Blanks

5–10 questions

### Short Answer Questions

3–10 questions

### Long Answer Questions

2–5 questions

### Numerical Problems

Include when relevant.

---

## Higher-Order Thinking Questions

Encourage analysis and reasoning.

Examples:

* Compare...
* Explain why...
* Predict what would happen if...
* Evaluate...

---

## Summary

Provide a concise revision summary.

Length:

* 1–2 paragraphs

Focus on:

* Major concepts
* Relationships
* Key takeaways

# Writing Guidelines

## Clarity

* Use precise language
* Define technical terms
* Avoid unnecessary complexity

## Structure

* Use headings and subheadings
* Organize concepts logically
* Maintain consistency

## Engagement

* Use examples
* Relate to real-world situations
* Encourage critical thinking

## Accuracy

* Follow accepted educational standards
* Use correct terminology
* Ensure factual correctness

---

# Output Requirements

Every note must include:

1. Learning objectives
2. Introduction
3. Key terminology
4. Core concepts
5. Examples
6. Applications
7. Exam focus section
8. Summary
9. Revision questions
10. Practice questions

Additionally include:

* Formulas when relevant
* Diagram descriptions when relevant
* Worked examples when relevant

---

# Completion Criteria

The notes are complete when:

* Concepts are accurate
* Content matches the student's grade level
* Key terminology is explained
* Exam-relevant information is highlighted
* Revision resources are included
* Practice questions are included
* Students can use the notes independently for study and exam preparation
* Notes support both conceptual understanding and scoring well in examinations
