# Solar System Vocabulary: Key Words to Know

Here are the important "space words" you will hear when learning about our Solar System. Understanding these words will make you a true space expert!

---

### 1. Solar System
* **What it means:** The Sun and all the objects that travel around it, including planets, moons, asteroids, and comets.
* **Easy memory tip:** "Solar" means Sun. So, the Solar System is the Sun's family system!

### 2. Star
* **What it means:** A giant, glowing ball of hot gas in space that makes its own light and heat.
* **Easy memory tip:** Our Sun is actually a star! It only looks bigger and brighter because it is much closer to us than other stars.

### 3. Planet
* **What it means:** A large, round object in space that travels in a path around a star.
* **Easy memory tip:** Planets do not make their own light; they reflect the light of the Sun.

### 4. Orbit
* **What it means:** The circular or oval-shaped path that an object in space takes around another object.
* **Easy memory tip:** Earth orbits the Sun, and the Moon orbits Earth. Think of it like walking in a circle around a flagpole.

### 5. Gravity
* **What it means:** An invisible pulling force that pulls objects toward each other. It is what keeps your feet on the ground and keeps the planets from floating away into space.
* **Easy memory tip:** If you drop an apple, gravity is what makes it fall down to the floor.

### 6. Atmosphere
* **What it means:** The layer of gases surrounding a planet.
* **Easy memory tip:** Earth's atmosphere has the air we breathe and protects us from the Sun's harmful rays.

### 7. Gas Giant
* **What it means:** A very large planet that is made mostly of gases (like hydrogen and helium) and liquids, rather than solid rock.
* **Easy memory tip:** Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune are the four gas giants. You cannot walk on them because there is no solid ground!

### 8. Moon
* **What it means:** A natural rocky object that orbits (travels around) a planet. Moons are also called natural satellites.
* **Easy memory tip:** Earth has just one moon, but giant planets like Jupiter and Saturn have almost one hundred moons!

### 9. Asteroid
* **What it means:** A chunk of rock or metal in space, mostly found in the "Asteroid Belt" between Mars and Jupiter.
* **Easy memory tip:** They are like leftover building blocks from when the planets were made.

### 10. Comet
* **What it means:** A chunk of ice, dust, and frozen gases that travels around the Sun. When it gets close to the Sun, the ice melts and creates a long, glowing tail.
* **Easy memory tip:** Think of a comet as a "dirty space snowball."

### 11. Dwarf Planet
* **What it means:** An object in space that is round and orbits the Sun, but is too small to be called a full planet. Pluto is the most famous dwarf planet.
* **Easy memory tip:** "Dwarf" means small. They are mini-planets!
