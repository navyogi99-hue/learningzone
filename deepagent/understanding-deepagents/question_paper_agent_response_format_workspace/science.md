### **Question 41 (Physics: Units of Measurement / SI Units)**
Which of the following is the SI base unit of **Luminous Intensity**?
*   **A)** Lux
*   **B)** Lumen
*   **C)** Candela
*   **D)** Watt

**Correct Answer:** **C**

**Detailed Explanation:**
Luminous intensity measures the quantity of light emitted by a source in a particular direction per unit solid angle. The **Candela (cd)** is one of the seven fundamental/base units in the International System of Units (SI).
*   **Lux (lx)** is the SI derived unit of illuminance (luminous flux per unit area).
*   **Lumen (lm)** is the SI derived unit of luminous flux (the total amount of visible light emitted by a source).
*   **Watt (W)** is the SI derived unit of power (energy transfer per unit time).

### **Question 42 (Physics: Optics / Light / Mechanics)**
What is the nature of the image formed by a **convex mirror** when an object is placed at any finite distance in front of it?
*   **A)** Real and inverted
*   **B)** Real and erect
*   **C)** Virtual and inverted
*   **D)** Virtual and erect

**Correct Answer:** **D**

**Detailed Explanation:**
A **convex mirror** (also known as a diverging mirror) always forms a **virtual, erect (upright), and diminished (smaller than the object)** image. This remains true regardless of where the object is placed in front of the mirror. 
Because convex mirrors provide a wide, upright, and clear field of view, they are extensively used as rear-view (wing) mirrors in automobiles to help drivers see traffic behind them.

### **Question 43 (Chemistry: Chemical Names and Formulas of Everyday Items)**
What is the chemical name of the common household substance **'Washing Soda'**?
*   **A)** Sodium bicarbonate
*   **B)** Sodium carbonate
*   **C)** Calcium oxychloride
*   **D)** Sodium hydroxide

**Correct Answer:** **B**

**Detailed Explanation:**
**Washing soda** is the chemical compound **Sodium carbonate decahydrate** ($\text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3 \cdot 10\text{H}_2\text{O}$). In its anhydrous form, it is simply sodium carbonate ($\text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3$). It is used in laundry detergents, glass manufacturing, and water softening.
*   **Sodium bicarbonate** ($\text{NaHCO}_3$) is known as **Baking Soda**.
*   **Calcium oxychloride** ($\text{CaOCl}_2$) is known as **Bleaching Powder**.
*   **Sodium hydroxide** ($\text{NaOH}$) is known as **Caustic Soda**.

### **Question 44 (Chemistry: Acids, Bases, and Salts / pH scale)**
Which of the following natural organic acids is predominantly present in **tomatoes**?
*   **A)** Citric acid
*   **B)** Oxalic acid
*   **C)** Lactic acid
*   **D)** Tartaric acid

**Correct Answer:** **B**

**Detailed Explanation:**
**Tomatoes** are a rich source of **Oxalic acid** ($\text{H}_2\text{C}_2\text{O}_4$), which is a naturally occurring organic acid. While tomatoes also contain small amounts of citric and malic acids, school curricula and standard competitive examinations identify oxalic acid as the primary diagnostic organic acid in tomatoes.
*   **Citric acid** is present in citrus fruits like lemons, limes, and oranges.
*   **Lactic acid** is found in sour milk, yogurt, and curd.
*   **Tartaric acid** is found in grapes, tamarinds, and unripe bananas.

### **Question 45 (Chemistry: Metals, Non-metals, and Gases)**
Which of the following non-metals exists in a **liquid state** at standard room temperature?
*   **A)** Mercury
*   **B)** Bromine
*   **C)** Chlorine
*   **D)** Phosphorus

**Correct Answer:** **B**

**Detailed Explanation:**
**Bromine (Br)** is the only non-metallic element that is a liquid at standard room temperature and pressure. It is a reddish-brown, highly volatile liquid.
*   **Mercury (Hg)** is also liquid at room temperature, but it is a **metal**, not a non-metal.
*   **Chlorine (Cl)** is a gaseous non-metal.
*   **Phosphorus (P)** is a solid non-metal.

### **Question 46 (Biology: Vitamins, Minerals, and Deficiency Diseases)**
Deficiency of which of the following vitamins is responsible for the skeletal disorder **'Rickets'** in children?
*   **A)** Vitamin A
*   **B)** Vitamin C
*   **C)** Vitamin D
*   **D)** Vitamin K

**Correct Answer:** **C**

**Detailed Explanation:**
**Rickets** is a deficiency disease in children characterized by soft, weak, and deformed bones (such as bowed legs). It is caused by a deficiency of **Vitamin D**, which is crucial for the absorption of calcium and phosphorus from the digestive tract to build strong bones.
*   **Vitamin A** deficiency causes night blindness and xerophthalmia.
*   **Vitamin C** deficiency causes scurvy (bleeding gums).
*   **Vitamin K** deficiency leads to poor blood clotting and excessive bleeding.

### **Question 47 (Biology: Human Anatomy and Physiology)**
Which organ in the human body is primarily responsible for **filtering blood, removing metabolic waste products, and producing urine**?
*   **A)** Liver
*   **B)** Kidney
*   **C)** Heart
*   **D)** Spleen

**Correct Answer:** **B**

**Detailed Explanation:**
The **Kidneys** are the primary organs of the human excretory system. Their main function is to filter the blood to remove nitrogenous metabolic wastes (such as urea and uric acid), regulate water and mineral/electrolyte concentrations, and maintain acid-base balance, excreting the waste as urine.
*   The **Liver** detoxifies harmful chemicals, synthesizes proteins, and secretes bile.
*   The **Heart** pumps oxygenated and deoxygenated blood throughout the circulatory system.
*   The **Spleen** filters blood, destroys old red blood cells, and stores platelets.

### **Question 48 (Biology: Plant Kingdom & Photosynthesis)**
Which specialized vascular tissue in plants is responsible for transporting **water and dissolved minerals** from the roots upwards to the leaves?
*   **A)** Phloem
*   **B)** Xylem
*   **C)** Parenchyma
*   **D)** Sclerenchyma

**Correct Answer:** **B**

**Detailed Explanation:**
**Xylem** is the specialized vascular tissue responsible for the unidirectional conduction of water and inorganic nutrient ions (minerals) from the soil, absorbed through the roots, to the aerial parts (stems and leaves) of the plant.
*   **Phloem** is the vascular tissue responsible for transporting organic nutrients (the sugars produced during photosynthesis) from the source (leaves) to the sink areas (roots, fruits, and growing tips) in a bidirectional manner.
*   **Parenchyma** is a simple permanent tissue involved in storage and photosynthesis.
*   **Sclerenchyma** is a simple permanent tissue consisting of dead cells that provides structural strength and mechanical support.

### **Question 49 (Biology: Cell Biology)**
Which of the following cell organelles is commonly referred to as the **'Powerhouse of the Cell'**?
*   **A)** Ribosome
*   **B)** Lysosome
*   **C)** Mitochondrion
*   **D)** Golgi apparatus

**Correct Answer:** **C**

**Detailed Explanation:**
The **Mitochondrion** (plural: mitochondria) is known as the "powerhouse of the cell" because it is the primary site of aerobic cellular respiration. Within the mitochondria, organic nutrients are oxidized to release energy, which is temporarily stored in the form of **Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP)**, the chemical energy currency of the cell.
*   **Ribosomes** are the cellular engines responsible for protein synthesis.
*   **Lysosomes** are known as the "suicide bags" because they contain hydrolytic digestive enzymes that break down cellular debris and pathogens.
*   The **Golgi apparatus** is responsible for modifying, packaging, and sorting proteins and lipids for transport.

### **Question 50 (Environment/Ecology)**
Which of the following chemical compounds is primarily responsible for the **depletion of the stratospheric ozone layer**?
*   **A)** Carbon Dioxide ($\text{CO}_2$)
*   **B)** Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs)
*   **C)** Methane ($\text{CH}_2$ or $\text{CH}_4$)
*   **D)** Sulfur Dioxide ($\text{SO}_2$)

**Correct Answer:** **B**

**Detailed Explanation:**
**Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs)** are synthetic chemicals that were widely used in refrigerants, air conditioners, and aerosol propellants. When released, they slowly rise into the stratosphere, where high-energy solar ultraviolet (UV) radiation breaks them down, releasing free **chlorine atoms**. A single chlorine atom can break down thousands of ozone ($\text{O}_3$) molecules into oxygen molecules ($\text{O}_2$), leading to the depletion of the ozone layer and the formation of the "ozone hole."
*   **Carbon Dioxide** ($\text{CO}_2$) and **Methane** ($\text{CH}_4$) are major greenhouse gases causing global warming.
*   **Sulfur Dioxide** ($\text{SO}_2$) is a major contributor to acid rain.
