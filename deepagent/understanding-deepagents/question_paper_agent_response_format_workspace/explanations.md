## Section 1: Reasoning Explanations (Q1 - Q10)

### **Q1: Number Series**
* **Correct Option:** **C) 57**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  1. Let us find the difference between consecutive terms in the series:
     * $15 - 12 = 3$
     * $21 - 15 = 6$
     * $30 - 21 = 9$
     * $42 - 30 = 12$
  2. Notice the pattern in the differences: they are consecutive multiples of 3 ($3, 6, 9, 12$).
  3. Following this pattern, the next difference must be $15$ (which is $3 \times 5$).
  4. Add this difference to the last term to find the missing number:  
     $\text{Missing Number} = 42 + 15 = 57$.

### **Q2: Letter Series**

  2. Analyze the pattern of corresponding positions in each 3-letter term:
     * **First letter of each term:** 
       $B (2) \xrightarrow{+6} H (8) \xrightarrow{+6} N (14)$  
       Following the $+6$ pattern, the next first letter is $14 + 6 = 20$, which corresponds to **T**.
     * **Second letter of each term:** 
       $D (4) \xrightarrow{+6} J (10) \xrightarrow{+6} P (16)$

     * **Third letter of each term:** 
       $F (6) \xrightarrow{+6} L (12) \xrightarrow{+6} R (18)$  
       Following the $+6$ pattern, the next third letter is $18 + 6 = 24$, which corresponds to **X**.

### **Q5: Syllogism**
* **Correct Option:** **B) Only conclusion II follows**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  1. **Analyze Statement 1:** "All pens are markers." This means the circle representing "Pens" is completely enclosed inside the "Markers" circle.
  2. **Analyze Statement 2:** "Some markers are rulers." This indicates an intersection between the "Markers" circle and the "Rulers" circle. However, this intersection may or may not overlap with the "Pens" circle.
  3. **Evaluate Conclusion I ("Some pens are rulers"):** Because there is no definite information linking "Pens" and "Rulers", the overlap is possible but not guaranteed. Hence, Conclusion I does not definitely follow.
  4. **Evaluate Conclusion II ("Some markers are pens"):** Since all "Pens" are inside "Markers", the portion of "Markers" occupied by "Pens" represents an intersection. Therefore, some markers are definitely pens, making Conclusion II logically valid.
  5. Thus, only conclusion II follows.

### **Q7: Analogy**

* **Detailed Explanation:**

     * $25 = 5^2$

  2. Apply the same logic to the second pair ($49 : \text{?}$):

### **Q9: Seating Arrangement (Simple)**
* **Correct Option:** **B) A**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  1. Let us denote the 5 positions from left to right as **1, 2, 3, 4, 5**. All friends face North.
  2. From condition 3: "E is at the far left end of the row" $\rightarrow$ **Position 1 = E**.
     * Row: `E, _, _, _, _`
  3. From condition 4: "D is in the second position from the right" $\rightarrow$ **Position 4 = D**.
     * Row: `E, _, _, D, _`
  4. From condition 2: "C is sitting next to D". This leaves two options for C:
     * **Case 1:** C is at Position 3 $\rightarrow$ Row: `E, _, C, D, _`
     * **Case 2:** C is at Position 5 $\rightarrow$ Row: `E, _, _, D, C`
  5. Test **Case 1**:
     * The empty seats are Positions 2 and 5.
     * From condition 1: "A is sitting next to B". Since the remaining spots (2 and 5) are not adjacent, A and B cannot sit together. Thus, Case 1 is invalid.
  6. Test **Case 2**:
     * The empty seats are Positions 2 and 3. These are adjacent, allowing A and B to sit together.
     * From condition 5: "A is to the right of B". Between positions 2 and 3, Position 3 is to the right. Therefore, **Position 2 = B** and **Position 3 = A**.
     * This leaves the finalized row: `E, B, A, D, C`.
  7. Verify all remaining conditions:
     * C (5) is not sitting with E (1) $\rightarrow$ True.
     * A (3) is to the right of B (2) and E (1) $\rightarrow$ True.
  8. The middle position is Position 3, which is occupied by **A**.

### **Q10: Calendar**
* **Correct Option:** **C) Thursday**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  1. We calculate the total number of odd days between 15th August 2011 and 15th August 2013.
  2. **From 15th August 2011 to 15th August 2012:**
     * 2012 is a leap year.
     * Since this year-long interval crosses February 29, 2012, it contains 366 days.
     * Dividing 366 by 7 ($366 \div 7 = 52$ weeks with a remainder of 2) yields **2 odd days**.
  3. **From 15th August 2012 to 15th August 2013:**
     * 2013 is an ordinary year.
     * It contains 365 days.
     * Dividing 365 by 7 ($365 \div 7 = 52$ weeks with a remainder of 1) yields **1 odd day**.
  4. **Total odd days:** $2 + 1 = 3\text{ odd days}$.
  5. **Determine the day:** Add 3 days to Monday:
     * $\text{Monday} + 3\text{ days} = \text{Thursday}$.
  6. Therefore, 15th August 2013 was a **Thursday**.

## Section 2: Numerical Ability Explanations (Q11 - Q20)

### **Q11: Simplification / BODMAS**
* **Correct Option:** **B) 44**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  Following the BODMAS rule (Brackets, Of, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction):
  1. **Solve the innermost round parentheses `(...)`:**
     $$12 - 32 \div 4 = 12 - 8 = 4$$
     The expression becomes:
     $$180 \div 15 \times \left[ 24 - \left\{ 38 - 4 \times 4 \right\} \right] + 12.5\% \text{ of } 160$$
  2. **Solve the curly braces `{...}`:**
     $$38 - 4 \times 4 = 38 - 16 = 22$$
     The expression becomes:
     $$180 \div 15 \times \left[ 24 - 22 \right] + 12.5\% \text{ of } 160$$
  3. **Solve the square brackets `[...]`:**
     $$24 - 22 = 2$$
     The expression becomes:
     $$180 \div 15 \times 2 + 12.5\% \text{ of } 160$$
  4. **Solve the "Of" / Percentage part:**
     $$12.5\% \text{ of } 160 = \frac{12.5}{100} \times 160 = \frac{1}{8} \times 160 = 20$$
     The expression becomes:
     $$180 \div 15 \times 2 + 20$$
  5. **Perform Division and Multiplication from left to right:**
     $$180 \div 15 = 12$$
     $$12 \times 2 = 24$$
     The expression becomes:
     $$24 + 20$$
  6. **Addition:**
     $$24 + 20 = 44$$

### **Q12: Number System**
* **Correct Option:** **B) 9**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  For a number to be divisible by 72, it must be divisible by both 8 and 9 (since 8 and 9 are co-prime factors of 72).
  1. **Divisibility by 8:**
     The last three digits of the number ($y28$) must be completely divisible by 8.
     Let us check for the smallest single digit value of $y$ ($0 \le y \le 9$):
     * If $y = 0 \implies 028$ (not divisible by 8)
     * If $y = 1 \implies 128 \div 8 = 16$ (divisible by 8)
     Thus, the smallest possible value of $y$ is **1**.
  2. **Divisibility by 9:**
     The sum of all digits of the 9-digit number must be a multiple of 9.
     $$\text{Sum} = 8 + 4 + 7 + x + 5 + 6 + y + 2 + 8 = 40 + x + y$$
     Substitute the value of $y = 1$:
     $$\text{Sum} = 40 + x + 1 = 41 + x$$
     For $(41 + x)$ to be divisible by 9, $x$ must be **4** (since $41 + 4 = 45$, which is the nearest multiple of 9).
  3. **Calculate $(2x + y)$:**
     $$2x + y = 2(4) + 1 = 8 + 1 = 9$$

### **Q13: Percentage**
* **Correct Option:** **C) 25,000**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  Let the total number of enrolled voters be $V$.
  1. **Voters who cast their votes:**
     Since 10% did not vote, $90\%$ of voters cast their votes.
     $$\text{Votes Cast} = 0.90V$$
  2. **Valid votes:**
     Since 10% of the cast votes were invalid, $90\%$ of the cast votes are valid.
     $$\text{Valid Votes} = 90\% \text{ of } 0.90V = 0.90 \times 0.90V = 0.81V$$
  3. **Vote distribution between the two candidates:**
     * Successful candidate (Winner) = 54% of the valid votes.
     * Losing candidate = $(100\% - 54\%) = 46\%$ of the valid votes.
     * Margin of victory (majority) = $54\% - 46\% = 8\%$ of the valid votes.
  4. **Calculate total enrolled voters ($V$):**
     $$8\% \text{ of } \text{Valid Votes} = 1620$$
     $$0.08 \times 0.81V = 1620 \implies 0.0648V = 1620$$
     $$V = \frac{1620}{0.0648} = 25,000$$

### **Q14: Profit and Loss**
* **Correct Option:** **C) 28.8%**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  Let us assume:
  * Total quantity of goods = 100 units
  * Cost Price (CP) of each unit = Rs. 100
  * Total Cost Price (CP) = $100 \times 100 = \text{Rs. } 10,000$
  1. **Calculate Marked Price (MP) per unit:**
     Since the markup is 40%:
     $$\text{MP} = 100 + 40\% \text{ of } 100 = \text{Rs. } 140$$
  2. **Calculate Revenue from the first 60% of the goods:**
     Quantity sold = 60 units at MP.
     $$\text{Revenue}_1 = 60 \times 140 = \text{Rs. } 8,400$$
  3. **Calculate Revenue from the remaining 40% of the goods:**
     Quantity sold = 40 units at a 20% discount on MP.
     $$\text{Discounted Selling Price per unit} = 140 \times (1 - 0.20) = \text{Rs. } 112$$
     $$\text{Revenue}_2 = 40 \times 112 = \text{Rs. } 4,480$$
  4. **Calculate total Selling Price (SP) and Profit:**
     $$\text{Total SP} = \text{Revenue}_1 + \text{Revenue}_2 = 8,400 + 4,480 = \text{Rs. } 12,880$$
     $$\text{Overall Gain} = \text{Total SP} - \text{Total CP} = 12,880 - 10,000 = \text{Rs. } 2,880$$
     $$\text{Overall Gain \%} = \left( \frac{2,880}{10,000} \right) \times 100 = 28.8\%$$

### **Q15: Simple and Compound Interest**
* **Correct Option:** **C) Rs. 9,000**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  The standard formula for the difference ($D_3$) between compound interest and simple interest for 3 years is:
  $$D_3 = P \left( \frac{r}{100} \right)^2 \left( \frac{300 + r}{100} \right)$$
  Where $P$ = Principal sum, $r$ = Rate = 10%, $D_3$ = Difference = Rs. 279.
  1. **Substitute the given values into the formula:**
     $$279 = P \left( \frac{10}{100} \right)^2 \left( \frac{300 + 10}{100} \right)$$
     $$279 = P \left( \frac{1}{10} \right)^2 \left( \frac{310}{100} \right)$$
     $$279 = P \times \frac{1}{100} \times \frac{31}{10} \implies 279 = P \times \frac{31}{1000}$$
  2. **Solve for $P$:**
     $$P = \frac{279 \times 1000}{31}$$
     Since $31 \times 9 = 279$:
     $$P = 9 \times 1000 = \text{Rs. } 9,000$$

### **Q16: Ratio and Proportion**
* **Correct Option:** **A) Rs. 575**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  1. **Find the incomes of A, B, and C:**
     Let their incomes be $3k$, $7k$, and $4k$ respectively.
     Given, A's income = Rs. 2400.
     $$3k = 2400 \implies k = 800$$
     $$\text{Income of C} = 4k = 4 \times 800 = \text{Rs. } 3200$$
  2. **Find the expenditures of A, B, and C:**

     $$\text{A's expenditure} = \text{Income} - \text{Savings} = 2400 - 300 = \text{Rs. } 2100$$

     $$4x = 2100 \implies x = 525$$
     $$\text{Expenditure of C} = 5x = 5 \times 525 = \text{Rs. } 2625$$
  3. **Calculate savings of C:**
     $$\text{C's savings} = \text{C's income} - \text{C's expenditure}$$
     $$\text{C's savings} = 3200 - 2625 = \text{Rs. } 575$$

### **Q17: Time and Work**
* **Correct Option:** **C) 12 days**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  1. **Find the total work and efficiency:**
     Let the total work be the LCM of 15 and 10 = 30 units.
     $$\text{Efficiency of A} = \frac{30 \text{ units}}{15 \text{ days}} = 2 \text{ units/day}$$
     $$\text{Efficiency of B} = \frac{30 \text{ units}}{10 \text{ days}} = 3 \text{ units/day}$$
     $$\text{Combined efficiency of A and B} = 2 + 3 = 5 \text{ units/day}$$
  2. **Calculate work done in first 2 days:**
     $$\text{Work done together in 2 days} = 2 \times 5 = 10 \text{ units}$$
  3. **Find the remaining work:**
     $$\text{Remaining work} = 30 - 10 = 20 \text{ units}$$
  4. **Calculate time taken by A to complete remaining work:**
     Since B left, A completes the 20 units of work.
     $$\text{Time taken by A} = \frac{\text{Remaining work}}{\text{Efficiency of A}} = \frac{20}{2} = 10 \text{ days}$$
  5. **Find the total days taken:**
     $$\text{Total days} = \text{Days worked together} + \text{Days worked by A alone} = 2 + 10 = 12 \text{ days}$$

### **Q18: Speed, Time, and Distance**
* **Correct Option:** **D) 1250 meters**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  1. **Given Data:**
     * Initial distance between them = 250 meters = $0.25$ km.
     * Speed of thief ($S_t$) = 10 km/h.
     * Speed of policeman ($S_p$) = 12 km/h.
  2. **Calculate Relative Speed:**
     Since both are running in the same direction:
     $$\text{Relative Speed} = S_p - S_t = 12 - 10 = 2\text{ km/h}$$
  3. **Calculate time taken by the policeman to catch the thief:**
     $$\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Initial Distance}}{\text{Relative Speed}} = \frac{0.25\text{ km}}{2\text{ km/h}} = 0.125\text{ hours}$$
  4. **Calculate distance covered by the thief in this time:**
     $$\text{Distance covered by thief} = \text{Speed of thief} \times \text{Time}$$
     $$\text{Distance} = 10\text{ km/h} \times 0.125\text{ h} = 1.25\text{ km}$$
     Converting to meters:
     $$1.25 \times 1000 = 1250\text{ meters}$$

### **Q20: Mensuration (2D)**
* **Correct Option:** **B) 616 cm²**
* **Detailed Explanation:**
  1. **Find the side and perimeter of the square:**
     $$\text{Area of square} = a^2 = 484\text{ cm}^2 \implies \text{Side (a)} = \sqrt{484} = 22\text{ cm}$$

  3. **Calculate the area of the circle:**
     $$\text{Area of circle} = \pi r^2 = \frac{22}{7} \times 14 \times 14 = 616\text{ cm}^2$$

## Section 3: English Language Explanations (Q21 - Q30)

### **Q21: Synonym**
*   **Correct Option:** **B) Industrious**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   The word **'Diligent'** means having or showing care and conscientiousness in one's work or duties; hardworking. **'Industrious'** is a direct synonym as it also means diligent, hard-working, and active.
    *   **'Idle'** is an antonym (lazy/inactive); **'Careless'** is incorrect as it represents lack of care; **'Ignorant'** refers to lacking knowledge.

### **Q22: Antonym**
*   **Correct Option:** **A) Friendly**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   The word **'Hostile'** means showing or feeling opposition or dislike; unfriendly or antagonistic. Therefore, **'Friendly'** is its direct and perfect antonym.
    *   **'Aggressive'**, **'Unfavorable'**, and **'Belligerent'** are synonyms or related words representing conflict.

### **Q23: Spotting the Error**
*   **Correct Option:** **B) nor the students was**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   When two subjects are connected by **"neither... nor"**, the verb must agree with the subject closest to it. Here, the closer subject is **"the students"** (plural). Therefore, the singular verb **"was"** must be replaced with the plural verb **"were"** to be correct.

### **Q24: Fill in the Blanks - Prepositions**
*   **Correct Option:** **B) to**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   The verb **"adapt"** (when meaning to adjust to new conditions/environments) is collocated with the preposition **"to"** (e.g., *"adapt ourselves to the changing circumstances"*).

### **Q25: Fill in the Blanks - Subject-Verb Agreement**
*   **Correct Option:** **B) have**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   The noun phrase **"A large number of"** is followed by a plural countable noun ("candidates") and always takes a **plural verb**. Hence, **"have"** is correct to form the present perfect tense. Note that *"The number of"* takes a singular verb, whereas *"A number of"* takes a plural verb.

### **Q26: Idioms and Phrases**
*   **Correct Option:** **A) At the very last moment**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   The idiom **"at the eleventh hour"** is a highly common English expression that means doing something at the last possible moment, right before a deadline or right before it is too late to make a difference.

### **Q27: One-Word Substitution**
*   **Correct Option:** **A) Sinecure**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   **'Sinecure'** refers to a job or position that requires little or no active responsibility or work but yields a regular, profitable income.
    *   **'Honorary'** means unpaid; **'Voluntary'** means done by free will without pay; **'Truant'** is a student who skips school.

### **Q28: Correct Spelling**
*   **Correct Option:** **B) Accommodation**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   The correct spelling is **"Accommodation"** (double 'c', double 'm', and 'o' in the middle syllable: **A-c-c-o-m-m-o-d-a-t-i-o-n**).

### **Q29: Active/Passive Voice Conversion**
*   **Correct Option:** **B) A delicious meal was prepared by the chef for the guests.**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   The active sentence is in the simple past tense (*"prepared"*). The rule for converting simple past tense into passive voice is: **Subject (New) + was/were + past participle (V3) of the verb + by + Object (New)**. Since "A delicious meal" is singular, we use *"was prepared"*.

### **Q30: Direct/Indirect Speech Conversion**
*   **Correct Option:** **A) He asked me where I was going.**
*   **Detailed Explanation:**
    *   When converting a direct interrogative sentence starting with a question-word ("Where") into indirect speech:
      1. The reporting verb *"said to"* becomes *"asked"*.
      2. No conjunction like *"that"* is used.
      3. The interrogative word order (*"are you going"*) changes to assertive word order (*"I was going"*).
      4. The present continuous tense changes to past continuous tense.

## Section 4: General Knowledge of India Explanations (Q31 - Q40)

### **Q31: Indian Freedom Struggle / History**
* **Correct Option:** **B) 1916**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The Lucknow Pact was signed in December 1916 during the joint annual session of the Indian National Congress and the All-India Muslim League in Lucknow. Orchestrated by Bal Gangadhar Tilak and Mohammad Ali Jinnah, it marked a common constitutional platform demanding self-government from the British. It also saw the reunion of the Moderate and Extremist factions of the Congress.

### **Q32: Indian Constitution / Polity**
* **Correct Option:** **B) 44th Amendment Act**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  Originally, the Right to Property was a Fundamental Right protected under Article 19(1)(f) and Article 31. Because it created substantial legal bottlenecks for public infrastructure and land reforms, the **44th Constitutional Amendment Act of 1978** (passed during the Morarji Desai led Janata Party government) deleted it from Part III and made it a constitutional/legal right under **Article 300A**.

### **Q33: Indian Geography**
* **Correct Option:** **C) Brahmaputra**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The Brahmaputra River originates in the Angsi Glacier near Mount Kailash in Tibet, where it is known as the **Yarlung Tsangpo** (meaning 'The Purifier'). It flows eastward across southern Tibet, turns south around Mount Namcha Barwa, enters Arunachal Pradesh as the *Dihang* or *Siang*, flows through Assam as the *Brahmaputra*, and enters Bangladesh as the *Jamuna* before draining into the Bay of Bengal.

### **Q34: Indian Economy**
* **Correct Option:** **A) C. D. Deshmukh**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  Sir Chintaman Dwarkanath Deshmukh (C. D. Deshmukh) was the first Indian Governor of the Reserve Bank of India, serving from 1943 to 1949. Sir Osborne Smith was the first overall Governor of the RBI (appointed in 1935 when the bank was established), but he was an Australian/British banker.

### **Q35: Famous Indian Monuments & Art/Culture**
* **Correct Option:** **A) Narasimhadeva I**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The Sun Temple at Konark, Odisha, was built around 1250 CE by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. Designed as a monumental 24-wheeled chariot pulled by seven horses, the temple is dedicated to the Sun God (Surya). It is built of black granite and was called the "Black Pagoda" by early European sailors.

### **Q36: Classical / Folk Dances of India**
* **Correct Option:** **C) Assam**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  Sattriya is a classical dance form that originated in the monasteries (Sattras) of Assam. It was introduced in the 15th century CE by the great saint and social reformer Sankaradeva as a dance-drama to propagate the Vaishnavite Bhakti movement. It was recognized as a classical dance of India in 2000.

### **Q37: National Parks / Biosphere Reserves in India**
* **Correct Option:** **A) Meghalaya**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The Nokrek Biosphere Reserve is located in the West Garo Hills district of Meghalaya. Part of UNESCO's World Network of Biosphere Reserves, it is a crucial habitat for the Red Panda, Asian Elephant, and Capped Langur. It also serves as a critical gene pool sanctuary for rare, wild citrus species like *Citrus indica* (the mother of all citrus fruits).

### **Q38: Important Indian Awards / Honors**
* **Correct Option:** **A) G. Sankara Kurup**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The Jnanpith Award is India's highest literary honor, established in 1961. The first-ever recipient was the eminent Malayalam poet **G. Sankara Kurup** in 1965 for his poetry collection *Odakkuzhal* ("The Bamboo Flute").

### **Q39: Indian National Symbols & Firsts in India**
* **Correct Option:** **A) Bachendri Pal**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  **Bachendri Pal** became the first Indian woman to climb Mount Everest on 23 May 1984. She was awarded the Padma Shri in 1984 and the Padma Bhushan in 2019. Santosh Yadav was the first woman in the world to scale Everest twice, while Arunima Sinha was the first female amputee in the world to climb it.

### **Q40: Static GK of India**
* **Correct Option:** **B) Pakistan**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The Radcliffe Line is the international border boundary between India and Pakistan (which included East Pakistan, now Bangladesh). It was drawn by Sir Cyril Radcliffe, Chairman of the Boundary Commissions, and declared on 17 August 1947 during the partition of India. The McMahon Line separates India and China, while the Durand Line separates Pakistan and Afghanistan.

## Section 5: Basic Science Explanations (Q41 - Q50)

### **Q41: Units of Measurement / SI Units**
* **Correct Option:** **C) Candela**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  Luminous intensity measures the light power emitted by a source in a particular direction per unit solid angle. The **Candela (cd)** is its SI base unit. Lux is the SI unit of illuminance; Lumen is the SI unit of luminous flux; Watt is the SI unit of power.

### **Q42: Optics / Light / Mechanics**
* **Correct Option:** **D) Virtual and erect**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  A **convex mirror** always forms a virtual, erect, and diminished image of an object, regardless of where the object is placed in front of it. Due to this characteristic (and their wide field of view), they are universally used as rear-view mirrors in vehicles.

### **Q43: Chemical Names and Formulas of Everyday Items**
* **Correct Option:** **B) Sodium carbonate**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The chemical name of **Washing Soda** is Sodium carbonate ($\text{Na}_2\text{CO}_3 \cdot 10\text{H}_2\text{O}$). Sodium bicarbonate ($\text{NaHCO}_3$) is Baking Soda; Calcium oxychloride ($\text{CaOCl}_2$) is Bleaching Powder; Sodium hydroxide ($\text{NaOH}$) is Caustic Soda.

### **Q44: Acids, Bases, and Salts / pH scale**
* **Correct Option:** **B) Oxalic acid**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  **Tomatoes** naturally contain **Oxalic acid** ($\text{H}_2\text{C}_2\text{O}_4$) as their primary natural organic acid. Citric acid is found in lemons/oranges; Lactic acid is found in sour milk/curd; Tartaric acid is found in tamarind.

### **Q45: Metals, Non-metals, and Gases**
* **Correct Option:** **B) Bromine**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  **Bromine (Br)** is the only non-metal that is a liquid at standard room temperature. **Mercury (Hg)** is also liquid at room temperature, but it is a **metal**. Chlorine is a gaseous non-metal, and Phosphorus is a solid non-metal.

### **Q46: Vitamins, Minerals, and Deficiency Diseases**
* **Correct Option:** **C) Vitamin D**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  **Rickets** is a bone-weakening skeletal disease in children caused by a deficiency of **Vitamin D**, which is necessary for the absorption of calcium and phosphorus. Vitamin A deficiency causes night blindness; Vitamin C causes scurvy; Vitamin K causes bleeding disorders.

### **Q47: Human Anatomy and Physiology**
* **Correct Option:** **B) Kidney**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The **Kidneys** are the primary organs of the human excretory system, responsible for filtering blood, removing nitrogenous metabolic wastes (such as urea), and excreting them as urine. The liver detoxifies chemicals; the heart pumps blood; the spleen filters blood cells and stores platelets.

### **Q48: Plant Kingdom & Photosynthesis**
* **Correct Option:** **B) Xylem**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  **Xylem** is the plant vascular tissue responsible for conducting water and dissolved minerals from the roots upwards to the leaves in a unidirectional flow. **Phloem** is responsible for transporting the sugars produced during photosynthesis in a bidirectional flow.

### **Q49: Cell Biology**
* **Correct Option:** **C) Mitochondrion**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  The **Mitochondrion** is known as the "powerhouse of the cell" because it is the primary site of aerobic cellular respiration, which converts food nutrients into chemical energy in the form of Adenosine Triphosphate (ATP). Ribosomes synthesize proteins; Lysosomes degrade waste (suicide bags); the Golgi apparatus modifies/packages proteins.

### **Q50: Environment/Ecology**
* **Correct Option:** **B) Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs)**
* **Detailed Explanation:**  
  **Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs)** release free chlorine atoms when broken down by solar ultraviolet (UV) radiation in the stratosphere. These chlorine atoms catalyze the breakdown of ozone ($\text{O}_3$) molecules into oxygen ($\text{O}_2$), depleting the protective ozone layer. Carbon dioxide and methane are greenhouse gases, and sulfur dioxide is a major contributor to acid rain.
