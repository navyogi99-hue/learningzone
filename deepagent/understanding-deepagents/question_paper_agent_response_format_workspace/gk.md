# General Knowledge of India Questions & Explanations

### **Question 31: Indian Freedom Struggle / History**
**Question:** In which year was the historic Lucknow Pact signed between the Indian National Congress and the All-India Muslim League?
* **A)** 1912
* **B)** 1916
* **C)** 1920
* **D)** 1922

**Correct Answer:** **B) 1916**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
The Lucknow Pact was a landmark joint agreement signed in December 1916 during the annual sessions of both the Indian National Congress (INC) and the All-India Muslim League held in Lucknow. Under this pact, orchestrated largely by leaders like Bal Gangadhar Tilak and Mohammad Ali Jinnah, both parties agreed to present common constitutional demands to the British government for self-government. Significantly, the session also witnessed the historic reunion of the Moderate and Extremist factions of the Congress, which had split at the Surat session in 1907.

### **Question 32: Indian Constitution / Polity**
**Question:** By which of the following Constitutional Amendment Acts was the 'Right to Property' removed from the list of Fundamental Rights in India?
* **A)** 42nd Amendment Act
* **B)** 44th Amendment Act
* **C)** 52nd Amendment Act
* **D)** 86th Amendment Act

**Correct Answer:** **B) 44th Amendment Act**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
Originally, the Right to Property was a Fundamental Right protected under Article 19(1)(f) and Article 31 of the Constitution of India. However, because it created substantial legal bottlenecks for land reforms and public infrastructure development, the 44th Constitutional Amendment Act, enacted in 1978 during the Janata Party administration, abolished it as a Fundamental Right. It was reclassified as a legal/constitutional right under **Article 300A** in Part XII of the Constitution.

### **Question 33: Indian Geography**
**Question:** Which of the following major rivers is known as the 'Tsangpo' in Tibet before it enters India?
* **A)** Ganga
* **B)** Indus
* **C)** Brahmaputra
* **D)** Yamuna

**Correct Answer:** **C) Brahmaputra**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
The Brahmaputra River originates in the Angsi Glacier, situated on the northern side of the Himalayas near Lake Mansarovar in Tibet, where it is known as the **Yarlung Tsangpo**. It flows eastward across southern Tibet for about 1,700 km before turning sharply south around Mount Namcha Barwa to enter India's Arunachal Pradesh, where it is called the *Dihang* or *Siang*. It then flows through the Assam valley as the *Brahmaputra*, enters Bangladesh as the *Jamuna*, and merges with the Ganga (Padma) before discharging into the Bay of Bengal.

### **Question 34: Indian Economy**
**Question:** Who among the following was the first Indian Governor of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI)?
* **A)** C. D. Deshmukh
* **B)** Sir Osborne Smith
* **C)** L. K. Jha
* **D)** Benegal Rama Rau

**Correct Answer:** **A) C. D. Deshmukh**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
Sir Chintaman Dwarkanath Deshmukh (C. D. Deshmukh) was a distinguished Indian civil servant who served as the first Indian Governor of the Reserve Bank of India from 11 August 1943 to 30 June 1949. He went on to serve as the Union Finance Minister of India. While Sir Osborne Smith was the first-ever Governor of the RBI (appointed in 1935 when the bank was established), he was an Australian/British banker, making Deshmukh the first Indian to hold the prestigious post.

### **Question 35: Famous Indian Monuments & Art/Culture**
**Question:** The famous Sun Temple of Konark, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Odisha, was built by which ruler of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty?
* **A)** Narasimhadeva I
* **B)** Anantavarman Chodaganga
* **C)** Bhanudeva IV
* **D)** Kapilendra Deva

**Correct Answer:** **A) Narasimhadeva I**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
The iconic Sun Temple at Konark, located near Puri in Odisha, was built around 1250 CE by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. Designed in the form of a monumental, 24-wheeled chariot dragged by seven horses, the temple is dedicated to the Sun God (Surya). Built using black granite, it was historically referred to by European sailors as the "Black Pagoda" because it served as a dark landmark for navigation along the Bay of Bengal coast.

### **Question 36: Classical / Folk Dances of India**
**Question:** Sattriya, a recognized classical dance form of India, originated in which of the following states?
* **A)** Manipur
* **B)** Odisha
* **C)** Assam
* **D)** Kerala

**Correct Answer:** **C) Assam**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
Sattriya is one of the eight principal classical dance traditions of India, originating in the state of Assam. It was introduced in the 15th century CE by the great Assamese polymath, Vaishnavite saint, and social reformer Mahapurush Sankaradeva. Originally performed by male monks (called *Bhokots*) in monastic temples known as *Sattras* as a form of mythological dance-drama, it was officially recognized as a classical dance by the Sangeet Natak Akademi of India in the year 2000.

### **Question 37: National Parks / Wildlife Sanctuaries / Biosphere Reserves in India**
**Question:** In which of the following Indian states is the Nokrek Biosphere Reserve located?
* **A)** Meghalaya
* **B)** Mizoram
* **C)** Arunachal Pradesh
* **D)** Sikkim

**Correct Answer:** **A) Meghalaya**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
The Nokrek Biosphere Reserve is located in the West Garo Hills district of Meghalaya, in Northeast India. Included in UNESCO's World Network of Biosphere Reserves, Nokrek is celebrated for its dense, untouched evergreen forest canopy and is a major sanctuary for the Red Panda, the Asian Elephant, and the Capped Langur. Additionally, Nokrek serves as a key gene pool sanctuary for rare, wild citrus species, most notably the *Citrus indica* (commonly considered the progenitor of all cultivated citrus fruits).

### **Question 38: Important Indian Awards / Honors**
**Question:** Who was the first recipient of the prestigious Jnanpith Award, India's highest literary honor?
* **A)** G. Sankara Kurup
* **B)** Sumitranandan Pant
* **C)** Tarasankar Bandyopadhyay
* **D)** K. Shivaram Karanth

**Correct Answer:** **A) G. Sankara Kurup**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
The Jnanpith Award, established in 1961 by the Bharatiya Jnanpith trust, is India's highest literary honor. It is presented annually to authors for their outstanding contribution to literature in any of the 22 languages specified in the Eighth Schedule of the Indian Constitution, as well as English. The inaugural award was presented in 1965 to the celebrated Malayalam poet **G. Sankara Kurup** for his collection of poems titled *Odakkuzhal* ("The Bamboo Flute").

### **Question 39: Indian National Symbols & Firsts in India**
**Question:** Who was the first Indian woman to successfully climb Mount Everest, the world's highest peak?
* **A)** Bachendri Pal
* **B)** Santosh Yadav
* **C)** Premlata Agrawal
* **D)** Arunima Sinha

**Correct Answer:** **A) Bachendri Pal**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
**Bachendri Pal** became the first Indian woman to reach the summit of Mount Everest on 23 May 1984, just a day before her 30th birthday. For her historic achievement, she was honored with the Padma Shri in 1984 and later the Padma Bhushan in 2019. Among the other options: Santosh Yadav was the first woman in the world to scale Mount Everest twice, while Arunima Sinha became the first female amputee in the world to scale Mount Everest in 2013.

### **Question 40: Static GK of India**
**Question:** The 'Radcliffe Line' serves as the international boundary line between India and which of the following countries?
* **A)** China
* **B)** Pakistan
* **C)** Nepal
* **D)** Myanmar

**Correct Answer:** **B) Pakistan**

**Detailed Explanation:**  
The Radcliffe Line is the international border line that demarcated the partition of British India into India and Pakistan (which included East Pakistan, now modern-day Bangladesh). It was drawn by **Sir Cyril Radcliffe**, the Chairman of the Boundary Commissions, and was officially published on 17 August 1947. In comparison, the boundary between India and China is known as the *McMahon Line*, and the boundary between Pakistan and Afghanistan is called the *Durand Line*.
