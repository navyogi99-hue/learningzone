### **Answer Key & Explanations**

**Q21. Correct Answer: C**
* **Explanation:** When two subjects are connected by "neither... nor...", the verb must agree with the nearer subject. In this sentence, the nearer subject is "the students" (plural), so the auxiliary verb should be plural ("were") instead of singular ("was"). The correct sentence is: *"Neither the teacher nor the students were present..."*

**Q22. Correct Answer: A**
* **Explanation:** 
  * "Agree on" is used when people collectively come to a mutual decision, consensus, or agreement regarding a topic, plan, or solution.
  * "Agree with" is used for agreeing with a person or their opinion (e.g., *I agree with you*).
  * "Agree to" is used for giving consent to a proposal, plan, or action (e.g., *He agreed to the terms*).

**Q23. Correct Answer: B**
* **Explanation:** The word "honorable" begins with a silent 'h' and is pronounced with an initial vowel sound (/ˈɒn.ər.ə.bəl/). Therefore, the indefinite article "an" is grammatically correct.

**Q24. Correct Answer: C**
* **Explanation:** When two actions happened in the past, the action that completed first is expressed in the past perfect tense ("had + V3"), while the subsequent action is expressed in the simple past tense ("reached"). Since the patient died before the doctor arrived, "had died" is the correct tense.

**Q25. Correct Answer: B**
* **Explanation:** "Benevolent" means well-meaning, generous, and kindly. Hence, "Kind" is the exact synonym. "Cruel" and "Hostile" are antonyms, while "Miserly" means cheap or hoarding money.

**Q26. Correct Answer: B**
* **Explanation:** "Frugal" means thrifty, sparing, or economical with regard to money or food. Its antonym is "Extravagant," which means spending money or using resources in a wasteful, lavish, or excessive manner.

**Q27. Correct Answer: B**
* **Explanation:** The idiom "call it a day" means to stop doing a particular activity, to finish working, or to retire.

**Q28. Correct Answer: B**
* **Explanation:** An "insolvent" person (bankrupt) is legally unable to pay their outstanding debts. "Solvent" is the antonym (able to pay debts). "Pauper" refers to a very poor person, and "Spendthrift" refers to someone who spends money excessively or wastefully.

**Q29. Correct Answer: A**
* **Explanation:** This is a first conditional sentence expressing a realistic future scenario. In conditional clauses (the "if" clause), the simple present tense ("studies") is used instead of the future tense ("will study"), while the main clause uses the simple future ("will pass").

**Q30. Correct Answer: A**
* **Explanation:** Latin adjectives ending in "-ior" (such as senior, junior, superior, inferior, prior, anterior, posterior) are followed by the preposition "to" instead of the conjunction "than" when making comparisons. Thus, "senior than" must be corrected to "senior to".
