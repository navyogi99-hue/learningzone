### Answer Key & Explanations

* **Q31 — Answer: B (Gopal Krishna Gokhale)**  
  * **Explanation:** Gopal Krishna Gokhale, a prominent moderate leader of the Indian National Congress, founded the *Servants of India Society* in Pune, Maharashtra, in 1905. The primary objective of the society was to train missionaries for the service of India and promote education, social reform, and civic welfare.

* **Q32 — Answer: A (Narmada)**  
  * **Explanation:** The Sardar Sarovar Dam is a massive concrete gravity dam built on the Narmada River near Kevadia in the state of Gujarat. It is one of the largest water resources projects in India, supplying drinking water and electricity to four states: Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Rajasthan.

* **Q33 — Answer: C (Article 32)**  
  * **Explanation:** Article 32 of the Constitution of India gives citizens the right to petition the Supreme Court directly for the enforcement of their Fundamental Rights through various writs (such as *Habeas Corpus*, *Mandamus*, etc.). Dr. B.R. Ambedkar famously referred to Article 32 as the "very soul of the Constitution and the very heart of it."

* **Q34 — Answer: B (Manipur)**  
  * **Explanation:** Keibul Lamjao National Park is located in the Bishnupur district of Manipur and is situated on Loktak Lake. It is the only floating national park in the world, famous for its unique floating decomposing plant materials called *phumdis*, and serves as the last natural refuge of the endangered *Sangai* (brow-antlered deer), which is also the state animal of Manipur.

* **Q35 — Answer: B (Samudragupta)**  
  * **Explanation:** The prominent colonial historian Vincent Arthur Smith referred to Samudragupta as the "Napoleon of India" because of his brilliant military campaigns, vast conquests, and undefeated record. His achievements and personality are elaborately documented in the famous *Prayag Prashasti* (Allahabad Pillar Inscription) composed by his court poet Harishena.

* **Q36 — Answer: C (35 years)**  
  * **Explanation:** According to Article 58 of the Constitution of India, a candidate must meet specific eligibility conditions to run for the presidency. These include being a citizen of India, having completed the age of 35 years, and being qualified for election as a member of the Lok Sabha (House of the People).

* **Q37 — Answer: A (NITI Aayog)**  
  * **Explanation:** The Union Government replaced the 65-year-old socialist-era Planning Commission of India with the **NITI Aayog** (*National Institution for Transforming India*) on January 1, 2015. It serves as a policy think-tank aiming to foster cooperative federalism by involving State Governments in economic policy-making.

* **Q38 — Answer: C (Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan)**  
  * **Explanation:** Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan (popularly known as "Frontier Gandhi" or "Badshah Khan") was awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1987, making him the first non-citizen recipient of the honor. Nelson Mandela was the second non-citizen recipient (awarded in 1990). *Note: Mother Teresa was awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1980, but she had already become a naturalized Indian citizen in 1950.*

* **Q39 — Answer: B (Gujarat)**  
  * **Explanation:** Emperor Akbar built the monumental Buland Darwaza (the highest gateway in the world, standing at 54 meters) in 1601 at Fatehpur Sikri to celebrate his decisive military victory and annexation of the rich province of Gujarat in 1573.

* **Q40 — Answer: D (Gujarat)**  
  * **Explanation:** Gujarat has the longest coastline among all Indian states, spanning approximately 1,600 km, owing to its many indentations and gulfs (Gulf of Khambhat and Gulf of Kutch). Andhra Pradesh has the second-longest coastline (approx. 974 km).
