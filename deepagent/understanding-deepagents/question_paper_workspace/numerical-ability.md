## Answer Key & Explanations

### Q11. Correct Answer: B (172)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. According to the VBODMAS rule, first solve the "of" operator:
   $$\frac{3}{5} \text{ of } 160 = \frac{3}{5} \times 160 = 3 \times 32 = 96$$
2. Next, solve the percentage:
   $$24\% \text{ of } 450 = \frac{24}{100} \times 450 = 24 \times 4.5 = 12 \times 9 = 108$$
3. Find the square root:
   $$\sqrt{1024} = 32$$
4. Substitute these values back into the original expression:
   $$96 + 108 - 32 = 204 - 32 = 172$$

### Q12. Correct Answer: B (8)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. A number is completely divisible by 72 if it is divisible by both 8 and 9 (since 8 and 9 are co-prime).
2. **Divisibility rule of 8:** The number formed by the last three digits ($74x$) must be divisible by 8.
   Testing single-digit values of $x$:
   - For $x = 4$, we have $744 \div 8 = 93$ (completely divisible). 
   - No other single digit satisfies this condition. Thus, $x = 4$.
3. **Divisibility rule of 9:** The sum of all digits must be divisible by 9.
   Substitute $x = 4$ into the number: $5432y1744$.
   $$\text{Sum of digits} = 5 + 4 + 3 + 2 + y + 1 + 7 + 4 + 4 = 30 + y$$
   The smallest multiple of 9 greater than or equal to 30 is 36.
   $$30 + y = 36 \implies y = 6$$
4. Find the value of $(5x - 2y)$:
   $$5(4) - 2(6) = 20 - 12 = 8$$

### Q13. Correct Answer: B (Rs. 20,000)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. Let the total monthly income of the man be Rs. $x$.
2. He spends 20% on house rent:
   $$\text{Remaining Income} = (100\% - 20\%) \text{ of } x = 0.8x$$
3. He spends 15% of the remaining on children's education:
   $$\text{Remaining Income} = (100\% - 15\%) \text{ of } 0.8x = 0.85 \times 0.8x = 0.68x$$
4. He spends 30% of the remaining on food and medicine:
   $$\text{Final Savings} = (100\% - 30\%) \text{ of } 0.68x = 0.70 \times 0.68x = 0.476x$$
5. Equate the savings to the given value:
   $$0.476x = 9520$$
   $$x = \frac{9520}{0.476} = \text{Rs. } 20,000$$

### Q14. Correct Answer: C (20%)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. Let the Cost Price (CP) of the goods be Rs. 100.
2. The shopkeeper marks his goods 35% above the CP:
   $$\text{Marked Price (MP)} = 100 + 35 = \text{Rs. } 135$$
3. He makes a profit of 8%:
   $$\text{Selling Price (SP)} = \text{CP} + \text{Profit} = 100 + 8 = \text{Rs. } 108$$
4. Calculate the discount offered:
   $$\text{Discount} = \text{MP} - \text{SP} = 135 - 108 = \text{Rs. } 27$$
5. Calculate the discount percentage:
   $$\text{Discount Percentage} = \frac{\text{Discount}}{\text{MP}} \times 100 = \frac{27}{135} \times 100 = \frac{1}{5} \times 100 = 20\%$$

### Q15. Correct Answer: C (750)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. Let the number of 50-paise, 25-paise, and 10-paise coins be $5k$, $8k$, and $12k$ respectively.
2. Convert the face values into Rupees:
   - Value from 50-paise coins $= 5k \times 0.50 = 2.5k \text{ Rs.}$
   - Value from 25-paise coins $= 8k \times 0.25 = 2.0k \text{ Rs.}$
   - Value from 10-paise coins $= 12k \times 0.10 = 1.2k \text{ Rs.}$
3. Find the total sum of these values:
   $$\text{Total Value} = 2.5k + 2k + 1.2k = 5.7k \text{ Rs.}$$
4. Equate to the given total value:
   $$5.7k = 171 \implies k = \frac{171}{5.7} = 30$$
5. Calculate the total number of coins:
   $$\text{Total coins} = 5k + 8k + 12k = 25k$$
   $$\text{Total coins} = 25 \times 30 = 750$$

### Q16. Correct Answer: C (62.5 kg)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. Total number of students in the class $= 15$.
2. The increase in the average weight $= 1.5 \text{ kg}$.
3. Calculate the total net weight added to the class:
   $$\text{Net Weight Increase} = 15 \times 1.5 = 22.5 \text{ kg}$$
4. Since the overall weight increased, the weight of the new student must exceed that of the replaced student:
   $$\text{Weight of new student} = \text{Weight of replaced student} + \text{Net Weight Increase}$$
   $$\text{Weight of new student} = 40 \text{ kg} + 22.5 \text{ kg} = 62.5 \text{ kg}$$

### Q17. Correct Answer: C (9 days)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. Let the total work be the LCM of 12 and 18, which is 36 units.
2. Determine their individual efficiencies (work per day):
   - Efficiency of A $= \frac{36}{12} = 3 \text{ units/day}$
   - Efficiency of B $= \frac{36}{18} = 2 \text{ units/day}$
3. A leaves 3 days before the completion of the work, meaning B worked completely alone for the final 3 days.
4. Work completed by B alone in the last 3 days:
   $$\text{Work done by B} = 3 \text{ days} \times 2 \text{ units/day} = 6 \text{ units}$$
5. Remaining work done by A and B together before A left:
   $$\text{Remaining work} = 36 - 6 = 30 \text{ units}$$
6. Combined daily efficiency of A and B $= 3 + 2 = 5 \text{ units/day}$.
7. Number of days they worked together:
   $$\text{Days together} = \frac{30 \text{ units}}{5 \text{ units/day}} = 6 \text{ days}$$
8. Total duration of the work:
   $$\text{Total days} = \text{Days together} + \text{Days alone by B} = 6 + 3 = 9 \text{ days}$$

### Q18. Correct Answer: C (24 years)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. Under Simple Interest, the interest earned is directly proportional to time ($SI \propto T$).
2. Let the principal amount be $P$.
   - In 8 years, the sum becomes $3P$. Therefore, Simple Interest earned ($SI_1$) $= 3P - P = 2P$ in $T_1 = 8$ years.
   - We want the sum to become $7P$. Therefore, the required Simple Interest ($SI_2$) $= 7P - P = 6P$ in $T_2$ years.
3. Apply the proportionality ratio:
   $$\frac{SI_1}{SI_2} = \frac{T_1}{T_2}$$
   $$\frac{2P}{6P} = \frac{8}{T_2} \implies \frac{1}{3} = \frac{8}{T_2}$$
   $$T_2 = 24 \text{ years}$$

### Q19. Correct Answer: B (Rs. 2,522)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. Given Principal ($P$) $=$ Rs. 16,000.
2. For interest compounded half-yearly:
   - Number of half-years ($n$) $= 1 \frac{1}{2} \text{ years} \times 2 = 3$ half-years.
   - Half-yearly rate ($r$) $= \frac{10\%}{2} = 5\%$ per half-year.
3. Calculate the total Amount ($A$):
   $$A = P \left(1 + \frac{r}{100}\right)^n = 16000 \left(1 + \frac{5}{100}\right)^3$$
   $$A = 16000 \left(\frac{21}{20}\right)^3 = 16000 \times \frac{9261}{8000}$$
   $$A = 2 \times 9261 = \text{Rs. } 18,522$$
4. Calculate the Compound Interest ($CI$):
   $$CI = A - P = 18522 - 16000 = \text{Rs. } 2,522$$

### Q20. Correct Answer: B (24 seconds)
**Step-by-step Explanation:**
1. Convert the speed of the train from km/h to m/s:
   $$\text{Speed} = 72 \text{ km/h} = 72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 20 \text{ m/s}$$
2. When crossing a pole, the train covers a distance equal to its own length:
   $$\text{Length of train} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 20 \text{ m/s} \times 15 \text{ seconds} = 300 \text{ meters}$$
3. To cross a platform of 180 meters, the train must cover its own length plus the platform length:
   $$\text{Total distance} = 300 \text{ m} + 180 \text{ m} = 480 \text{ meters}$$
4. Calculate the time taken to cover this total distance:
   $$\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Total distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{480 \text{ meters}}{20 \text{ m/s}} = 24 \text{ seconds}$$
